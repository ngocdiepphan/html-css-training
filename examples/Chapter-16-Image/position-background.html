Magnolia

Magnolia soulangeana was originally bred by the French gardener Étienne Soulange-Bodin (1774–1846), a retired cavalry officer in Napoleon's army, at his chateau at Fromont near Paris. He crossed Magnolia denudata with M. liliiflora in 1820, and was impressed with the early flowering of the first seedling in 1826
